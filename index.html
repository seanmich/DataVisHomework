World Education Statistics

A visualization by Sean Michael

“Education is the passport to the future, for tomorrow belongs to those who prepare for it today.” – Malcolm X

I wanted to visualize the answer to the question:
"How much do governments spend on Education for their country?

As we scroll through the years, we notice some countries' governments increase the amount they spend on education. One notable example being Brazil, who increased their spending from 3.9% in 2000 to 6.1% in 2018

Effect on Primary Enrolment Rate

To see an effect that government spending on education might have, I plotted government spending on education against primary enrolment rate. The intuition being that the more a government spends on education, the more people have access to education.

But we can see some interesting outliers such as Singapore having a 99% enrolment rate while spending only 2.8% of it's budget on education.

Total Labor Force per Country

One would think that just because the government spends a lot of money on education, it won't mean that their labor force will necessarily be large.

In fact, something we see with the labor force chart is that not every country who spends a lot on education has a good labor force.
For example, despite spending 6% on education, Brazil has fewer labor force than the US, who only spends 3%.

University Rankings 2018-2021

Our of curiosity, I wanted to know what were the rankings of the top 10 universities in the world and how does their rank change over years.

Surprisingly, the world's top university, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, is consistently top 1 over the span of 5 years.

Another interesting note is that no other universities rise to the top 10 during that span of 5 years.
